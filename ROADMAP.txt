To support mobile devices and cloud accessibility, we need to transition away from local storage.

2. **Backend/Frontend Hosting**: Deploy the FastAPI app and static files to **Vercel** or **Render**.
   - Vercel works great for serverless Python functions + static frontends.

## 🚀 Phase 2: Solving the "Laziness" Problem

You asked for ideas on how to prevent yourself from forgetting to use the app or getting lazy. Here are the best strategies, ranked from easiest to hardest:

### 1. The PWA (Progressive Web App) Approach 🌟 *(Highly Recommended)*
Instead of building a full Android app from scratch (which takes a lot of time), we can turn this current web app into a **PWA**.
- **How it works:** You "install" the website directly to your phone's home screen.
- **Why it's great:** It looks and feels exactly like a native app, and we can easily send **Web Push Notifications** straight to your phone.

### 2. Telegram / Discord Bot Integration 🤖 *(Easiest automation)*
Instead of dealing with email servers, we can wire the backend to a simple Telegram Bot.
- Every morning at 9 AM, your bot messages you: *"Hey! You have 15 practice questions due today for Computer Graphics. Click here to start: [Link]"* 
- You already check your messaging apps daily, so it naturally fits your habits.

### 3. Email Automation 📧
We can use a free service like **Resend** or **SendGrid** coupled with a daily Cron job.
- Sends you a daily summary: *"Today's tasks: 1 Video to Review, 10 Flashcards due."*
- **Pros:** Reliable. 
- **Cons:** Emails often get ignored or routed to spam promotions over time.

### 4. Native Android / iOS App 📱
Building a dedicated mobile app using React Native or Flutter, and using Firebase/Supabase for push notifications. 
- **Pros:** Maximum control, app store presence.
- **Cons:** High development overhead just for personal use.
